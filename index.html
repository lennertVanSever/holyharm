Why I Made This Site

Hey there, thanks for stopping by. If you're wondering why this website exists, it's simple: I'm deeply concerned about the issue of child abuse within the Catholic Church. We can't afford to look the other way any longer.

Let's be honest, the numbers you see here are likely not 100% accurate. Child abuse is often underreported, so there are many untold stories. But we have to start somewhere, and the data we do have is eye-opening enough to warrant serious attention and action.

And just so you know, I'm Catholic myself. Creating this site isn't about attacking my own faith. It's about facing a significant issue within our Church. We can't keep ignoring it. This is a call for awareness, dialogue, and meaningful change.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What is the source of the data?

The data is collected from various credible sources including governmental reports, academic studies, and reputable news outlets. Every data point is carefully verified before being included.

2. Why is my country's data wrong or not available?

We do our best to gather accurate information, but sometimes the data for a specific country might be missing or not quite right. This could happen for a variety of reasons, such as limited public information or inconsistencies in the sources we have. If you know of better or more current data for a country, we'd really appreciate it if you could share it with us on our GitHub repository.

3. How can I contribute to the project?

The project is open-source and hosted on GitHub. Anyone can open an issue, express concerns, or ask questions about the data. Contributions in the form of code or data are also welcome. You can access the GitHub repository.
